Sample Lesson: Online Safety

1. Warm-Up (5 min)

Start with a quick question:
"Have you ever shared your name or photo online?"
Use a poll or simple hand raise to begin discussion and get students thinking.

2. Video (5–7 min)

Play a short, story-based animated or narrated video:
Title: "Anaya's Online Adventure"
A story of a girl who shares personal information online and faces unexpected problems.

3. Discussion (10 min)

  • What went wrong in the story?
  • What should Anaya have done instead?

Then, show two screenshots — one from a real website, one from a fake/scam site — and ask them to spot the difference.

4. Activity (15 min)

Do a group activity or worksheet:

Game: "Personal Info or Not?"

Students sort items like:

  • Name
  • Aadhaar number
  • Phone number
  • Pet's name
  • Favorite color

Then, they create a Strong Password using a fun, memorable pattern (like combining emojis, initials, and numbers).

5. Quick Quiz (5–7 min)

Ask 5 multiple choice questions (Google Form or printed):

  • What is phishing?
  • What makes a password strong?
  • What information should you never post online?
  • What should you do if you face cyberbullying?
  • Who can you talk to if something feels wrong online?

6. Closing (3 min)

Ask each student to share:
"One thing I'll do differently online after this class."

Hand them a small takeaway card or digital poster titled:
"5 Rules of Online Safety"
