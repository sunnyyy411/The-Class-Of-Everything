Life skills for Classes 4–8. Teaching what schools don't.

What We Do

We teach life skills like communication, financial literacy, emotional intelligence, digital safety, and more — through fun, interactive, and psychology-backed methods.

The Problem

90% of students don't know how to budget or save money.

70% are afraid of public speaking or expressing themselves.

Only 1 in 10 students can identify fake news or online scams.

Most children struggle to manage emotions or resolve conflicts peacefully.

Our Unique Method

We don't just teach life skills — we make them stick.

Traditional education relies on memorization. We use research-backed, experiential learning that transforms knowledge into lifelong abilities.

Gap-Filling Curriculum

  • • Practical over theoretical: Instead of just defining "interest rates," students run a mock café to experience loans and profits.
  • • Age-specific challenges:
    Classes 4–5: Emotional awareness, basic money habits
    Classes 6–8: Digital safety, critical thinking, career exploration
  • • Local relevance: We use Indian examples like UPI scams, bullying cases, and local heroes to connect with students' real lives.

Research-Based Learning

  • • Students retain 80% with activity-based learning vs. just 10% in passive lectures (NEP 2020)
  • • Learning by teaching improves understanding by 50% (University of London, 2023)
  • • Spaced repetition: Core skills are revisited regularly to ensure long-term memory

The 4-Pillar Framework

Pillar How It Works Example
Stories Real-world narratives to spark curiosity A teen avoids an online scam → Teaches digital safety
Activities Games, role-plays, and simulations "Budgeting Olympics" → Kids allocate ₹5000 to needs/wants
Discussions Peer-led debates and classroom reflections "Is homework more important than sleep?" → Builds critical thinking
Reflections Journals or group sharing to internalize learning "3 things I learned about my emotions today"

Measurable Impact

  • • Pre/Post Assessments: Can the child now spot a phishing email or make a budget?
  • • Skill Badges: Earned for specific competencies (e.g., "First Aid Champ", "Digital Detective")
  • • Parent Reports: Real-life examples of skills being used at home

Why It Works

  • • No boring lectures — only interactive, fun formats (like escape rooms for problem-solving)
  • • A safe space to fail — practice real-world decisions risk-free
  • • Peer learning — kids learn best from each other through teamwork
